Personal Portfolio Website

  • quick overview

    My personal portfolio website has always been intended as a library or even an archive of my most ambitious and exciting projects. However, I have tried to go beyond that, and make its design as clean, fresh and good-looking as possible.

Design of a website

The design, throughout the various stages of the project, remained pretty consistent. I decided to create the aurora-styled background for the website right from the start. This really helped me to figure out the whole look of a website. After that, all I needed was to decide on minor details: which types of fonts I should use and how to efficiently design the project and article grid on the home page.

First, I tried to make an uneven, complex grid. But, after a few considerations, I settled on making a simple, clean 2-column grid, both for the projects and the articles. In my opinion, a simple grid fits in better with the overall website aesthetics and composition. Speaking of which, I attempted to structure the website’s layout according to the basic principles of composition.

Coding a portfolio

Coding the website was pretty easy. The only thing I struggled with was the implementation of responsive font sizes. And since I didn’t need to control both dimensions in a grid, I decided to use flexbox.

Overall thoughts on creating a portfolio website

As somebody once said, making a design for your own website is the hardest project you can take on as a designer. And I completely agree with that statement. The whole process of designing and then redesigning the look of a website took about 80% of the overall time dedicated to this project. But after all, I am really proud of how both the design and the coding turned out.
